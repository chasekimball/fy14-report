Kimball Campbell Report

Fiscal Year 2014

On October 1, 2013, the U.S. Government shut down. It was bad. However, for the two of us -- a pair of young government workers named Heather and Chase -- the forced time off came as a welcome reprieve. Several months of particularly busy times at work had left us no time for a serious conversation we knew we wanted to have. So on the first day of the shutdown, we escaped the city for the hills of West Virginia to talk about life, love, and our future. Two days later, after a long walk, we proposed to each other on the banks of the Potomac River. We were married in Albuquerque, New Mexico on March 15, 2014.

The alignment of the federal fiscal year with the timeline of our relationship inspired this report. Chase's love of data brought it into being. And our lives provided the content. Using a variety of data sets, The Kimball Campbell FY 2014 Report documents our relationship from 10/1/2013 to 9/30/2014. In short: we travel extensively, enjoy dinner with friends, and communicate often.

Next year's version of this pie chart should be much more boring.

Phone Calls

Phone calls between us were longer and more frequent before in the evenings and in the days leading right up to our wedding. Once we were married there was less need to talk over the phone -- except for the trips Heather took to Miami in June.

Emails

Our pattern of emailing each other changed dramatically once we were married. Not shown here is the increase in post-it note usage after March 14.

Texts

We continued to text each other frequently after our marriage, though there was less need to text early in the morning or late at night.

Air Travel


We travelled extensively in FY14. Memorable trips included a family vacation to Iceland, friends' weddings in Seattle and Boulder, a whirlwind trip to Puerto Rico, and of course our wedding in Albuquerque! We continue to be each other's favorite travel companions.
